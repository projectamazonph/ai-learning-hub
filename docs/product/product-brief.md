# Product Brief

## Product

**Name:** AI Ready PH Learning Hub  
**Category:** applied AI education and work simulation  
**Founder:** Ryan Roland Dabao  
**Initial market:** beginner Filipino virtual assistants and adjacent freelancers  
**Expansion market:** business owners, managers, teams, and regional learning communities

## Product sentence

AI Ready PH helps people turn AI confusion into practical skills, repeatable workflows, and proof they can safely deliver useful work.

## Problem

Most beginner AI education fails in predictable ways:

- It teaches a changing tool interface instead of transferable judgment.
- It gives prompt recipes without task framing, context selection, or quality control.
- It rewards completion while learners remain unable to perform realistic work.
- It encourages trust in confident output without source verification.
- It ignores privacy, client data, copyright, prompt injection, cost, and approval boundaries.
- It produces certificates with no evidence an employer or client can inspect.

Virtual assistants feel an added pressure: they fear replacement while clients increasingly expect faster delivery. Freelancers struggle to convert casual AI use into a defensible service. Business owners see potential but lack a safe, measurable adoption path.

## Product answer

The hub combines short instruction with realistic practice:

1. A readiness diagnostic establishes the learner's role, goal, current ability, risk level, and time.
2. Practical AI Foundations builds the shared mental model.
3. Role paths apply those skills to VA, freelance, or business work.
4. Versioned labs use synthetic data, explicit success criteria, and visible rubrics.
5. Learners revise weak work and save strong evidence.
6. A Skills Passport shows competency, evidence, and verification state.
7. Capstones receive deterministic checks, calibrated evaluation, and human moderation.

## Audiences

### Primary beta audience

Filipino VAs who are new to AI or use it inconsistently. They need confidence, safe client-work patterns, portfolio evidence, and a clearer path to higher-value responsibility.

### Secondary individual audience

Freelancers who want to package AI-assisted work as an outcome-based service rather than sell prompts or unreviewed output.

### Expansion audience

Business owners and managers who need opportunity discovery, workflow redesign, team policy, pilot measurement, and adoption visibility.

## Core learning paths

- AI-Powered Virtual Assistant.
- AI Freelancer.
- AI for Business Owners.
- No-Code AI Automation and Agents, after the core product proves demand.

## Product principles

1. Outcomes before tools.
2. Practice before confidence.
3. Verification is part of the task.
4. Synthetic data by default.
5. Human judgment remains visible.
6. Mobile is a full classroom.
7. Content carries freshness metadata.
8. Certification requires evidence.
9. Model usage is metered and cost-capped.
10. External actions require explicit approval.

## North-star metric

**Weekly verified work artifacts produced by active learners.**

Minutes watched and prompts sent are supporting activity metrics, not the product outcome.

## Beta definition of success

- 30 learners complete onboarding.
- At least 80% reach a first artifact.
- At least 60% complete one full lab.
- Median time to first artifact is below 20 minutes.
- At least 70% report that feedback changed how they performed the task.
- No unresolved high-severity privacy, payment, tenant, or AI-safety defect.
- Observed inference cost supports the planned gross margin.
