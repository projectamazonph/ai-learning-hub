# Lesson Standard

## Objective

Create short, practical lessons that change learner behavior. The source content must remain separate from rendering mechanics.

## Target length

- Core reading or video: 5 to 10 minutes.
- Guided example: 3 to 7 minutes.
- Practice: 5 to 20 minutes.
- Full micro-lesson: normally below 25 minutes.

## Required lesson sequence

1. **Outcome:** what the learner will be able to do.
2. **Why it matters:** realistic consequence in work.
3. **Concept:** plain-language explanation.
4. **Weak attempt:** common beginner behavior.
5. **Diagnosis:** why the attempt fails.
6. **Improved method:** step-by-step correction.
7. **Guided practice:** one small task.
8. **Verification:** checklist or knowledge check.
9. **Transfer:** where else the method applies.
10. **Reflection:** one judgment question.

## Supported content blocks

- `concept`.
- `example`.
- `weak-attempt`.
- `diagnosis`.
- `checklist`.
- `callout`.
- `comparison`.
- `reflection`.
- `knowledge-check`.
- `guided-input`.
- `lab-launch`.
- `download`.
- `source-list`.

Each block has a stable identifier for progress, analytics, localization, and resume state.

## Writing rules

- Use plain English.
- Define jargon at first use.
- Use short sentences and direct verbs.
- Explain the reason behind a step.
- Prefer realistic Philippine and international work contexts.
- Use synthetic names and data.
- Avoid promises that AI removes the need for review.
- Never imply one prompt or tool is always best.
- State when to stop, verify, ask, or escalate.
- Separate fact, recommendation, and uncertainty.

## Tool-specific content

Tool interface walkthroughs are volatile supplements, not the core lesson.

They require:

- Authoritative source.
- Product and feature name.
- Reviewed date.
- Region or plan limitation when relevant.
- Screenshot version.
- Expected review cadence of 14 to 30 days.

## Localization

- English is the canonical source.
- Translation uses stable block identifiers.
- Taglish versions preserve technical terms when translation reduces clarity.
- Regional-language expansion must receive human review by a fluent speaker.
- Examples may localize names, currency, work context, and tone without changing competency or scoring rules.

## Completion rules

Completion is declared per lesson type:

- Reading: required blocks viewed plus knowledge check or reflection.
- Video: required watch threshold plus check; seeking is allowed.
- Practice: valid saved response.
- Quiz: submitted attempt.
- Lab: required run state.

Scrolling to the bottom is never sufficient by itself.
