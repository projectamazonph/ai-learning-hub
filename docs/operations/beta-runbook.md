# Founding Beta Runbook

## Cohort

- 30 learners.
- Primary: beginner Filipino VAs.
- Secondary: adjacent freelancers.
- Mobile participation intentionally represented.

## Required beta journey

1. Account activation.
2. Readiness diagnostic.
3. Recommended path review.
4. First-win Prompt Workbench scenario.
5. One Practical AI Foundations module.
6. Weekly feedback.
7. Exit survey or interview.

## Before opening

- Consent, privacy, terms, support, and beta expectations published.
- Synthetic scenario and safety copy reviewed.
- Activation, first-lab, save, error, feedback, and cost events verified.
- Provider daily cap and kill switch tested.
- Support owner and response window active.
- Rollback and incident runbooks ready.
- Mobile critical path inspected.

## Weekly operating rhythm

### Monday

- Review activation, completion, errors, AI cost, and support.
- Select one learning or UX hypothesis.

### Tuesday to Thursday

- Conduct learner sessions and interviews.
- Review failed and abandoned lab runs through privacy-safe evidence.
- Ship only small changes with tests and release notes.

### Friday

- Cohort summary.
- Decide keep, change, remove, or investigate.
- Update content, product, and backlog evidence.

## Interview prompts

- Show me what you expected before clicking.
- What made you hesitate?
- Which feedback changed the work?
- Which score did you distrust?
- What would you have delivered without the lab?
- Where did mobile make the task harder?
- What would you use next week in real work?
- What should be removed?

## Daily watch

- Diagnostic completion.
- First-lab start and completion.
- Save conflict and failure.
- Provider error and cost.
- Safety blocks.
- Support requests.
- Mobile abandonment.

## Success gate

- 80% reach first artifact.
- 60% complete one lab.
- Median first artifact below 20 minutes.
- 70% say feedback changed their method.
- No unresolved high-severity issue.
- Cost supports product economics.

## Stop conditions

Pause affected capability for:

- Private-data exposure.
- Forged or incorrect entitlement.
- Unreconciled credit runaway.
- Repeated lost work.
- Unsafe model behavior without containment.
- Assessment producing untrustworthy pass decisions.

## Close

- Export final metric and qualitative summary.
- Identify strongest value and highest-friction step.
- Resolve critical defects.
- Confirm MVP scope changes through `DECISIONS.md`.
- Thank participants and communicate next access state.
