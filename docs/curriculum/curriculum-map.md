# Curriculum Map

## Learning architecture

Every learner follows:

1. Readiness diagnostic.
2. AI Ready in 90 Minutes.
3. Practical AI Foundations.
4. One role path.
5. Portfolio or business capstone.
6. Evidence review and Skills Passport update.

Lessons use a micro-learning loop: **understand, inspect, practice, verify, reflect, transfer**.

## Free course: AI Ready in 90 Minutes

### Outcome

Produce one reviewed artifact and understand the learner's next path.

### Units

1. AI without the fog machine.
2. What AI is good at and where it fails.
3. The seven-part task brief.
4. Safe inputs and synthetic data.
5. First-win Prompt Workbench scenario.
6. Verification checklist.
7. Skills baseline and recommended plan.

## Practical AI Foundations

**Effort:** 10 to 12 hours  
**Modules:** 10  
**Lessons:** 30  
**Labs:** 7  
**Checks:** 10  
**Capstone:** one reviewed end-to-end workflow

### Module 0: Orientation and baseline

**Outcome:** use the hub safely and define a measurable learning goal.

- Lesson 0.1: How the learning system works.
- Lesson 0.2: Readiness diagnostic and personal goal.
- Lesson 0.3: Synthetic practice data and learning agreement.
- Check: identify safe and unsafe training inputs.

### Module 1: AI without the fog machine

**Outcome:** explain what modern AI can and cannot reliably do.

- Lesson 1.1: Models, assistants, tools, automations, and agents.
- Lesson 1.2: Generation, prediction, retrieval, and tool use.
- Lesson 1.3: Hallucination, uncertainty, and confident mistakes.
- Lesson 1.4: When not to use AI.
- Check: choose the correct role for AI in six work situations.

### Module 2: Task framing

**Outcome:** convert a vague request into a complete task brief.

- Lesson 2.1: Objective and observable result.
- Lesson 2.2: Audience and success criteria.
- Lesson 2.3: Inputs, context, constraints, and exclusions.
- Lesson 2.4: Clarifying questions and assumptions.
- Lab: Prompt Workbench, weekly client update.
- Check: diagnose four incomplete briefs.

### Module 3: Prompting as work design

**Outcome:** create and improve reusable instructions.

- Lesson 3.1: Instructions, context, examples, and output structure.
- Lesson 3.2: Decomposition and staged work.
- Lesson 3.3: Critique, revision, and comparison.
- Lesson 3.4: Reusable templates without rigid prompt formulas.
- Lab: Prompt Workbench, research summary.
- Check: revise a weak prompt and explain the change.

### Module 4: Files and context

**Outcome:** select and prepare useful source material without oversharing.

- Lesson 4.1: Context quality beats context volume.
- Lesson 4.2: Preparing documents, notes, and tables.
- Lesson 4.3: Context windows, chunking, and loss of detail.
- Lesson 4.4: Sensitive data and redaction.
- Lab: Data Safety Clinic, client source pack.
- Check: classify and prepare a mixed file set.

### Module 5: Research and verification

**Outcome:** distinguish supported facts, inferences, opinions, and unknowns.

- Lesson 5.1: Research questions and evidence plan.
- Lesson 5.2: Primary, authoritative, and weak sources.
- Lesson 5.3: Citation and claim tracing.
- Lesson 5.4: Contradictions, missing evidence, and uncertainty.
- Lab: Research and Verification, market brief.
- Check: audit ten claims against a source pack.

### Module 6: Everyday knowledge work

**Outcome:** use AI for common work while maintaining human quality control.

- Lesson 6.1: Email, updates, and stakeholder communication.
- Lesson 6.2: Documents, summaries, and action plans.
- Lesson 6.3: Spreadsheet cleanup, categories, formulas, and analysis.
- Lesson 6.4: Presentations and visual briefs.
- Lab: Brief-to-Deliverable Studio.
- Check: select workflow and QA steps for four deliverables.

### Module 7: Output quality assurance

**Outcome:** find and correct the defects that matter before delivery.

- Lesson 7.1: Correctness and evidence.
- Lesson 7.2: Completeness and instruction fit.
- Lesson 7.3: Tone, format, accessibility, and usability.
- Lesson 7.4: Revision notes and audit trail.
- Lab: Output QA Lab.
- Check: classify defects and prioritize fixes.

### Module 8: Workflows, automation, and agents

**Outcome:** design a repeatable process with appropriate human control.

- Lesson 8.1: From prompt to workflow.
- Lesson 8.2: Human, AI, and system responsibilities.
- Lesson 8.3: Triggers, decisions, approvals, failures, and rollback.
- Lesson 8.4: Assistant versus automation versus agent.
- Lab: Workflow Canvas in demo mode.
- Check: map the approval and failure path for a recurring task.

### Module 9: Responsible and secure use

**Outcome:** handle privacy, copyright, bias, malicious instructions, and approvals.

- Lesson 9.1: Data classes and provider boundaries.
- Lesson 9.2: Copyright, attribution, and transformation.
- Lesson 9.3: Bias, harm, and inappropriate delegation.
- Lesson 9.4: Prompt injection and untrusted content.
- Lesson 9.5: Team policy and escalation.
- Lab: Data Safety Clinic, risky request.
- Check: respond to six safety and governance scenarios.

### Foundation capstone

Given a messy synthetic request and mixed source pack, the learner must:

- Clarify the task.
- Build a complete brief.
- Select an AI role and workflow.
- Produce the deliverable.
- Verify evidence and quality.
- Record revisions and unresolved uncertainty.
- Explain privacy and approval decisions.

## AI-Powered Virtual Assistant

**Effort:** 16 to 20 hours  
**Modules:** 8  
**Capstone:** simulated client week

### VA-1: Client task intake

- Clarify requests without slowing the client down.
- Convert messages into briefs and acceptance criteria.
- Identify missing access, data, deadline, and approval.
- Lab: five-message task triage.

### VA-2: Communication and coordination

- Email drafting and reply review.
- Meeting notes, actions, owners, and dates.
- Weekly updates and escalation.
- Calendar and coordination boundaries.
- Lab: client communication desk.

### VA-3: Research and reporting

- Build research questions and source packs.
- Produce evidence-linked briefs.
- Separate fact, inference, and recommendation.
- Lab: competitor research brief.

### VA-4: Spreadsheet and operations support

- Clean structure and labels.
- Categorize and summarize.
- Explain formulas and checks.
- Turn findings into an operator report.
- Lab: messy operations tracker.

### VA-5: Content operations

- Brand context and content briefs.
- Repurpose without flattening meaning.
- Check claims, tone, and channel fit.
- Maintain a review and approval flow.
- Lab: multi-channel content pack.

### VA-6: SOP and process documentation

- Observe a process.
- Separate policy, procedure, checklist, and exception.
- Add owners, evidence, and change control.
- Lab: SOP repair workshop.

### VA-7: Quality, privacy, and escalation

- QA under time pressure.
- Handle client secrets and risky requests.
- Communicate uncertainty and errors.
- Escalate rather than bluff.
- Lab: red-team client day.

### VA-8: Career proof

- Explain AI use without exaggeration.
- Build portfolio evidence.
- Discuss workflow and QA in interviews.
- Set client expectations and boundaries.
- Lab: portfolio and interview defense.

### VA capstone: simulated client week

The learner receives synthetic inbox messages, meeting notes, a research request, a spreadsheet, a content brief, and a process problem. Required evidence:

- Task intake board.
- Two client communications.
- Research brief with claim trace.
- Cleaned tracker and summary.
- One SOP improvement.
- QA checklist and issue log.
- Weekly client update.
- Reflection on where AI was used, checked, rejected, or escalated.

## AI Freelancer

**Effort:** 14 to 18 hours  
**Modules:** 8  
**Capstone:** complete service package

1. Select a niche and measurable outcome.
2. Define inputs, exclusions, risks, and client responsibilities.
3. Price using effort, usage cost, review cost, risk, and value.
4. Build discovery, proposal, and onboarding assets.
5. Design delivery with approval checkpoints.
6. Manage revisions, evidence, and scope change.
7. Create safe case studies and portfolio proof.
8. Present and defend the service.

Capstone artifacts:

- Offer page.
- Discovery form.
- Proposal and statement of work.
- Delivery SOP.
- Sample artifact and rubric.
- AI usage and data policy.
- Case study.
- Cost and margin sheet.

## AI for Business Owners

**Effort:** 12 to 16 hours  
**Modules:** 8  
**Capstone:** 90-day adoption plan

1. Map business goals, constraints, and workflow bottlenecks.
2. Inventory recurring knowledge work.
3. Score opportunities by value, feasibility, risk, and adoption effort.
4. Redesign one workflow with human control.
5. Define policy, approved tools, data classes, and incident response.
6. Build a pilot and success metrics.
7. Train the team and manage workflow change.
8. Review results and set the next 90 days.

Capstone artifacts:

- Opportunity inventory and scorecard.
- Selected pilot brief.
- Current and proposed workflow.
- Risk register.
- AI use policy.
- ROI estimate.
- Training and adoption plan.
- 90-day roadmap.

## No-Code AI Automation and Agents

This path is deferred until the shared lab engine, safety controls, and role paths are stable.

Modules:

1. Workflow and data contracts.
2. Trigger, action, condition, and state.
3. APIs and webhooks for non-developers.
4. Retrieval and knowledge systems.
5. Agent roles, tools, memory, and permissions.
6. Human approval and external actions.
7. Testing, failure, monitoring, cost, and rollback.
8. Production-readiness capstone.
