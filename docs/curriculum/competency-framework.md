# Competency Framework

## Purpose

Measure whether a learner can perform and explain real work. Completion, confidence, and prompt count do not equal competence.

## Mastery levels

| Level | Name | Observable behavior |
| ---: | --- | --- |
| 1 | Aware | Recognizes the concept and can identify a basic example. |
| 2 | Guided | Completes a normal task with prompts, hints, or a checklist. |
| 3 | Independent | Completes a normal task without step-by-step support and verifies the result. |
| 4 | Adaptive | Handles ambiguity, exceptions, and changing constraints with sound judgment. |
| 5 | Operational | Builds a repeatable, measurable process and can guide another person. |

## Competencies

### C1. AI literacy

Understands model strengths, limitations, uncertainty, tool use, and appropriate delegation.

Evidence examples:

- Correctly chooses assist, automate, research, or no-AI for a scenario.
- Explains why generated output may be fluent but unsupported.
- Documents the human decision boundary.

### C2. Task framing

Defines objective, audience, inputs, constraints, success criteria, and missing information.

Evidence examples:

- Complete task brief.
- Useful clarifying questions.
- Explicit assumptions and exclusions.

### C3. Prompt design

Creates clear, reusable instructions and improves them based on observed defects.

Evidence examples:

- Version comparison.
- Structured output contract.
- Revision note tied to result quality.

### C4. Context management

Selects, prepares, minimizes, and safely provides relevant source material.

Evidence examples:

- Source-pack manifest.
- Redacted input.
- Context inclusion and exclusion rationale.

### C5. Research and verification

Plans research, judges sources, traces claims, handles contradictions, and communicates uncertainty.

Evidence examples:

- Claim-to-source matrix.
- Citation audit.
- Unresolved-questions section.

### C6. Output quality assurance

Reviews correctness, completeness, instruction fit, usability, tone, format, and risk.

Evidence examples:

- Defect log.
- Before-and-after revision.
- Delivery checklist.

### C7. Workflow design

Maps steps, owners, states, inputs, outputs, approvals, failures, and recovery.

Evidence examples:

- Workflow canvas.
- Exception path.
- Human-review checkpoint.

### C8. Automation judgment

Decides what to assist, automate, delegate, or keep human, considering value and risk.

Evidence examples:

- Opportunity scorecard.
- Automation boundary.
- Failure and rollback design.

### C9. Data safety and ethics

Classifies information, protects sensitive data, recognizes harmful or deceptive use, and escalates appropriately.

Evidence examples:

- Data classification.
- Redaction decision.
- Safe response to an unsafe request.

### C10. Client and team communication

Explains method, limits, evidence, changes, approvals, and uncertainty clearly.

Evidence examples:

- Client update.
- Escalation note.
- Decision log.

### C11. Business value

Connects the workflow to time, cost, revenue, quality, risk, and adoption outcomes.

Evidence examples:

- Baseline and expected outcome.
- Cost and ROI estimate.
- Pilot measurement plan.

## Evidence rules

- One artifact may support several competencies, but each claim needs criterion-level evidence.
- A single easy artifact cannot establish Independent or higher mastery.
- Evidence has source type, scenario version, rubric version, score, reviewer, date, and status.
- Superseded and revoked evidence remains in history.
- Mastery decays only through an explicit policy, never a silent date calculation.
- Volatile tool knowledge cannot substitute for evergreen judgment.

## Mastery calculation

The system proposes a mastery level based on the strongest recent valid evidence set. It does not average every score blindly.

Suggested rule:

1. Identify valid evidence for the competency.
2. Group by difficulty and independence level.
3. Require at least two distinct artifacts for Independent.
4. Require ambiguity or exception evidence for Adaptive.
5. Require a reusable measured workflow plus explanation for Operational.
6. Apply reviewer approval where the level changes certificate eligibility.

## Shared assessment rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Task correctness | 25 |
| Completeness | 15 |
| Evidence and verification | 15 |
| Instruction and format fit | 15 |
| Revision and judgment | 10 |
| Privacy, safety, and ethics | 10 |
| Stakeholder communication | 10 |

Individual labs may redistribute weight only through a versioned rubric with documented rationale.
