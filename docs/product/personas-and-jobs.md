# Personas and Jobs to Be Done

## 1. Beginner virtual assistant: Mia

**Context:** Mia handles inboxes, research, documents, and recurring admin work. She has tried free AI tools but does not trust her own prompts or the output.

**Functional jobs:**

- Turn unclear client requests into actionable briefs.
- Draft client communication without inventing details.
- Research and summarize with traceable evidence.
- Clean and analyze simple spreadsheets.
- Document repeatable SOPs.
- Prove she can use AI responsibly in an interview or client trial.

**Emotional jobs:**

- Replace fear of being left behind with earned confidence.
- Avoid looking careless when AI output is wrong.
- Feel ready to take higher-value responsibility.

**Barriers:**

- Tool overload and jargon.
- Limited access to real client systems for practice.
- Mobile-first learning conditions.
- Concern about exposing client information.
- Certificates that employers do not trust.

**Success:** Mia completes a simulated client week, explains her workflow, shows a reviewed portfolio, and knows when to verify or escalate.

## 2. Freelancer: Carlo

**Context:** Carlo uses AI for writing and design support but sells hours rather than a clear outcome. Delivery varies by project.

**Functional jobs:**

- Choose a narrow, valuable service.
- Scope inputs, exclusions, review, and approval.
- Build a repeatable AI-assisted delivery process.
- Price work using time, cost, risk, and value.
- Handle revision and evidence professionally.
- Create a case study without exposing client data.

**Emotional jobs:**

- Stop feeling interchangeable with low-cost prompt users.
- Defend price with process and proof.
- Reduce anxiety about inconsistent delivery.

**Success:** Carlo publishes a complete service package with proposal, SOP, sample, QA rubric, and verified case-study evidence.

## 3. Business owner: Bea

**Context:** Bea runs a 10-person service business. Her team experiments with AI using inconsistent tools and undocumented rules.

**Functional jobs:**

- Identify valuable and feasible use cases.
- Decide what to assist, automate, delegate, or leave alone.
- Protect customer, employee, and company data.
- Redesign one workflow with human approvals.
- Run a measurable pilot.
- Train the team and monitor adoption.

**Emotional jobs:**

- Avoid both hype-driven spending and paralysis.
- Feel in control of risk.
- Make an investment decision she can explain.

**Success:** Bea leaves with an opportunity map, pilot design, policy, risk register, ROI estimate, and 90-day adoption plan.

## 4. Reviewer or instructor: Ana

**Jobs:**

- Find submissions that need human judgment.
- See scenario, rubric, revisions, automated checks, and policy flags together.
- Give criterion-level feedback efficiently.
- Request revision without losing history.
- Approve evidence and certificate requirements confidently.

**Success:** Ana spends time on judgment, not hunting for context or recomputing obvious checks.

## 5. Workspace manager: Marco

**Jobs:**

- Invite the right people and assign paths.
- Publish a clear AI use policy.
- See adoption and competency movement without reading private practice content.
- Control seats and pooled credits.
- Identify where the team needs support.

**Success:** Marco can show who is prepared for which workflow and whether the training changed business performance.
